Wedding Location

The wedding events will take place in the city of Haldwani, Uttarakhand. All events will take place in walking distance from the hotel, and a bus will be organized between Indira Gandhi International Airport and the hotel. We will update the page with the exact location once it has been set. You can read more about the different transportation options between the airport and Haldwani in the next section.

How to get there

Haldwani is located ~300 km from Indira Gandhi International Airport. The most comfortable ways to reach Haldwani are by flying to New Delhi and taking the train from there, or by flying to Pantnagar Aiport (35 - 40 km from Haldwani) and taking the train/taxi from there.

Choose any alternative travelling route you prefer. You only need to concern yourself with getting to the wedding before the programme starts, which is the 24th of March at 18:00, when the pre-wedding "welcome" party starts. If you also cannot make that, the formal wedding ceremonies start on the 25th of March at 10:00 in the morning.

  • The closest airport to Haldwani is Pantnagar Airport (PGH), located ~30km from the city. There are direct flights from Indira Gandhi International Airport in New Delhi to Pantnagar Airport, which leave every morning, according to current scheduling, at around 10 AM in the morning and cost around 2000 Indian Rupee (26 EUR / 244 NOK) one way.

  • Trains from New Delhi to Haldwani go three times every day and they take between 5 - 8 hours. A first class ticket (incl. proper seat, airconditioning, warm meal) costs about 100 NOK / 10 EUR. If you want to take the train, you should book this in advance as the trains are usually fully booked quite fast. Reservations are made generally up to 120 days in advance for all classes and all trains, and can be done via IRCTC's web site.

  • For the guests who prefer travelling by bus, we will arrange a private bus from the airport in New Delhi to Haldwani, which will leave on the morning of 24th of March at 9 AM in the morning. You would have to arrive at New Delhi's Indira Gandhi International Airport before 7 AM to go through customs and make it on time to the bus. The bus trip will take approximately 6 - 8 hours, including a long lunch break.

  • An easy way to travel around in India is to hire a driver and car. The cost varies upon the type of vehicle and the travelling distance. Normal rate is around 1.85 NOK / 0.2 EUR per km, but for longer distances, such as New Delhi to Haldwani, they charge you for both ways. In addtion, you have to pay 60 - 120 NOK / 6.5 - 13 EUR for the driver per day. There are many companies providing this kind of service. If you choose this option with a specific company, you'd be welcome to share your search results on Facebook.

    • As many people are planning their own individual holidays directly after the wedding, going in different directions, we are not arranging a bus back from Haldwani to Delhi. Feel free to ask us for tips about which places you could go to afterwards, or check the page about travelling tips in India.

    Dress code

    During an Indian wedding, the women usually wear traditional Indian clothing such as a 'Sari' or a 'Lehenga'. The female clothing is often very colourful, and there are no rules on which colours you can wear, although the bride usually wears something in the shade of red. The men usually wear a normal western suit or a 'Kurta'.

  • Sari

    A long piece of fabric wrapped around the body and over the shoulder. The fabric is usually ~6m long and is worn with a blouse

  • Lehenga

    A form of skirt worn together with a matching blouse and shawl. Usually has a lot of embroidery of different kinds.

  • Kurta

    The straight-cut kurta is a loose shirt falling either just above or somewhere below the knees of the wearer. Kurtas are worn both as casual everyday wear and as formal dress.

    • However, you do not need to wear traditional Indian clothes during the wedding if you don't want to. Wearing a western dress or suit is also perfectly fine. For those like Geoffrey who can't handle even the slightest heat, it's OK to take off your tie and suit jacket.

    If you wish to get yourself traditional Indian clothes, we advise you to find your local Indian store. For the guests coming from the Oslo area, Grønland has a good selection of clothing. If you have some days in India before the wedding, it is (obviously) also possible to buy traditional clothing there. Beware though that the female clothing often require some tailoring. It is also possible to order traditional Indian clothes online, for example via sites such as Nalli.com.

    Indian visa

    Make sure to arrange your visa on time and ensure your passport is valid for a long enough period. You can easily apply for an eVisa electronically through the government's official site; just follow the instructions at the bottom on the page! Applicants of the eligible eVisa countries/territories may apply online minimum 4 days in advance of the date of arrival with a window of 120 days. Example: If you are applying on the 1st of December, then the eVisa applicant can select an arrival date from the 5th of December to the 31st of March.
